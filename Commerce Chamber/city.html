The White City
Arequipa

Arequipa is everything you can dream of, it is the 'White City' of Peru. Its history, adventure, fabulous landscapes, fauna, gastronomy, art and much make the Villa de Nuestra Señora de la Asunta de Arequipa, the name with which it was founded, in 1540, the most beautiful city in the valley that is firmly guarded by three volcanoes. . Misti, Pichu Pichu and Chachani

Arequipa helps you change the routine of the city and breathe a bit of nature, you can appreciate the modern city from the outside, where traditions and ancestral places are still preserved, surrounded by beautiful landscapes full of green. From the viewpoints of Yanahuara, Sachaca, or Carmen Alto, you will be able to appreciate the fabulous scenarios that Arequipa offers you.

Magic places

Santa Catalina Monastery

Also known as the Santa Catalina Convent, it is more than 4 centuries old; Santa Catalina Convent was built on an area of 20 thousand square meters; Founded by María de Guzmán with the support of Viceroy Francisco Toledo, the place was chosen for its natural beauty, for the cozy climate and the arrangement of the unique material that is ashlar.

This Convent was built to house the daughters of the most distinguished families of Arequipa, the women who entered the Monastery as nuns were Creoles, mestizos, even daughters of curacas. It was only in 1964 that the first Spanish nuns entered; history also tells that poor nuns and ladies of the city entered. The Monastery of Santa Catalina in 1970 opened the doors for the entry of tourists.

The Arequipa Countryside

Is a spectacle of green color: the valley falls in love with its natural light, platforms, and bright colors, all with the imposing Misti in the background. Feel its essence is in the Sabandía Mill, built-in 1621. It is a place of particular charm, surrounded by trees, cacti, and various flowers. Wheat was crushed there four centuries ago on its great stone wheels.

Colca Canyon

Overflowing Andean vegetation surrounds one of the deepest canyons in the world: the Colca Canyon, the perfect setting for both extreme sports and the most peaceful rest, all under the watchful eye of the king of these skies, the condor. Viewpoints and hot springs; canoeing, mountaineering, or trekking; Sighting of the condor and other typical animals of the mountains in their natural habitat such as the alpaca, the vizcacha, or the puma, is what this valley with its privileged climate offers.

Delicious gastronomy

A shrimp chupe concentrates the flavors of the town with western ingredients. Likewise, the alternatives to satisfy your food whims are countless. The Arequipa Ocopa, an emblematic dish of the city with a pleasant flavor, the solterito de queso, which is a kind of fresh and light salad where beans, corn, onion, cheese, tomato, and chopped olive are combined. The emblematic dishes of the city are Arequipeño Adobo, Potato cake with stuffed red pepper, and Cuy Chactado, you will be completely satisfied, as they are forceful and are usually served in generous portions.

Industrialization in Arequipa

Until before the 1950s, Arequipa was characterized by its artisan character and producer of consumer goods. It became the second most industrialized city in Peru, thanks to the creation of the Industrial Park, which was born from a series of tasks for the Rehabilitation of Arequipa after the 1960 earthquake.

At that time, in addition to the Industrial Park, two or three more factories were built, and the Cemento Yura factory. Thus, by the end of the 70s, Arequipa had almost 80% of the gross industrial product of the southern region of Peru. In the time Arequipa has great development potential, so it is necessary to energize and decentralize the regional industry..
